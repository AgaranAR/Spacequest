Tech Insights

For the first time, scientists achieve successful training of a machine learning model in outer space.

In a groundbreaking milestone, scientists have successfully conducted the training of a
machine learning model in the expanse of outer space, within the confines of a satellite's
operational domain. This remarkable accomplishment holds the promise of facilitating instantaneous
monitoring and cognitive deliberations across an array of domains, encompassing the strategic realms of disaster management and ecological preservation.

Scottish geological formations poised to assume a pivotal role in forthcoming Mars space expedition.

Ancient rocks from the Isle of Rum in northwest Scotland are playing an important role in an
international space mission to discover more about Mars.

Toyota's Lunar Cruiser: Smooth Lunar Travels Enhanced by Fuel Cell Propulsion.

Toyota revealed this Friday it intends to employ regenerative fuel cell technology for a manned lunar
rover, the Toyota Lunar Cruiser.

Engineers test printed electronics in space

In an achievement of engineering prowess, hybrid printed circuits underwent successful testing
on the fringes of space during a sounding rocket mission launched
on April 25 from NASA's Wallops Flight Facility, situated near Chincoteague, Virginia.

A novel telescope concept employing advanced diffractive lenses could potentially exceed the capabilities of the James Webb telescope, ushering in a new era devoid of mirrors.

The lenses have other benefits, too. First, they are much easier and quicker to fabricate than mirrors and
can be made en masse. Second, lens-based telescopes work well even
when not aligned perfectly, making these telescopes easier to assemble and
fly in space than mirror-based telescopes, which require extremely precise alignment.
